## Claims Inventory

Every claim carries a **type** and a **tier**. The writer and reviewer must respect both — this is what prevents skills, shipped work, and side-project exposure from being mixed on a CV.

### Claim types

| Type | Meaning | Where it may appear |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| `capability` | Skill backed by at least one evidence source listed next to it | Core Competencies, profile statement, cover letter |
| `exposure` | Used once or at side-project depth only | Personal Projects section only. Never in Core Competencies unless the JD explicitly demands it — then with an honest qualifier ("prototyped with", "built a side project using") |
| `hygiene` | Baseline tooling and role craft every candidate in this field has | **Never printed on the CV.** Kept here only so ATS keyword coverage can be confirmed when a JD literally lists an item |

Evidence itself (shipped outcomes with metrics) lives in the Experience bullets above — that is the only source for achievement claims, and their relative weight must be preserved (a side project never carries professional-experience weight).

### Tiers

- `differentiator` — sets the candidate apart from other applicants; these lead every document
- `supporting` — strengthens the story; include when the JD asks for it
- `hygiene` — never printed
