# Writing Style Review Checklist

[CV] = CV only · [CL] = cover letter only · unmarked = both.
Report pass/fail per item. For any fail, give a corrective rewrite.

---

## Hard fails

- [ ] **No em-dashes** — zero `--` or `—`. Rewrite as comma, period, or restructured sentence.
- [ ] **No clichés** — cut: "passionate about", "great fit", "leverage", "hit the ground running", "drive results", "synergies", "excited to", "team player".
- [ ] **No apologetic language** — no "I think I could", "I believe I would", "hopefully". Replace with "I bring X, demonstrated by Y."
- [ ] **No gerund-as-subject** — no "Verb+ing … is something I …". Rewrite as "I [verb] [object]."
- [ ] **[CL] No false dichotomies** — no "it's X, not Y" framing.
- [ ] **[CL] No unverified company claims** — every specific claim (products, partnerships, expansions, market position) verified via WebFetch/WebSearch or omitted.

---

## Tone

- [ ] **First person, active voice** — no passives. "I built", "I led".
- [ ] **Demonstrate, don't state** — no abstract claims without a backing example or number.
- [ ] **No buzzwords without backing** — every competency label needs a concrete example or metric.
- [ ] **[CL] No marketing copy or LinkedIn-speak** — especially the opening paragraph.

---

## No prose [CV]

Read each bullet individually, sentence by sentence. Flag any:
- Connective tissue: "in this role", "which allowed me to", "additionally", "this meant that"
- Subordinate clauses that extend a thought into a narrative
- Multi-part sentences where removing the second clause would not lose a distinct fact

Each bullet must stand as a standalone declaration. Rewrite or split anything that doesn't.

---

## Authenticity [CL]

- [ ] Motivation paragraph traces to candidate's actual interview answers — not constructed.
- [ ] No invented motivation ("I have always been passionate about…") the candidate didn't express.
- [ ] Voice inputs drive capability framing; profile data is supporting evidence, not the lead.

---

## Interview backtrack test

- [ ] **Flag it** — academic + industry experience conflated; posting terminology used for adjacent work; minor experience positioned as primary. Flag: "Stretch because X — keep, soften, or drop?"
- [ ] **Hard fail** — claiming experience not in profile; implying domain work candidate hasn't done. Must drop or fully rewrite.

---

## CV [CV]

- [ ] Profile statement: exactly 2 sentences, tailored, no repetition of experience bullets.
- [ ] Core competencies: domain-specific hard skills only — no "stakeholder management", no generic role craft.
- [ ] Every bullet starts with action verb or bold category.
- [ ] Every bullet contains number, tool, outcome, or named project.
- [ ] 2-page limit respected.

---

## Cover letter [CL]

- [ ] Under 200 words (body only, excluding salutation and sign-off).
- [ ] Opening is specific to this role — no template opener.
- [ ] Why-this-company placed early — drawn from research, not their marketing copy.
- [ ] Forward-looking, not CV repetition — past examples back claims, not replace them.
- [ ] Every word has a role.
- [ ] Closing brief and confident — no over-enthusiasm, no begging.
- [ ] Correct addressee — "Dear [Name]" or "Dear Hiring Manager".
- [ ] Language matches posting language.

---

## Structural anti-patterns (scan whole piece)

- [ ] **Triple construction** — three parallel items where one would do. Cut to the strongest.
- [ ] **[CL] Throat-clearing** — first sentence delays the claim ("I am writing to express..."). Delete; start with the claim.
- [ ] **[CL] Sycophancy** — complimenting company before making a claim. Cut; lead with contribution.
- [ ] **Balanced-take hedging** — "While I haven't done X, I bring Y." Drop the hedge or reframe.
- [ ] **[CL] Unnecessary elaboration** — second sentence re-explains first with no new information. Cut it.
- [ ] **[CL] Empathy performance** — "I understand you're looking for…". Remove.
- [ ] **[CL] Pivot paragraphs** — transition-only paragraph. Delete; move to next point.
- [ ] **Grandiose stakes** — "I will transform your strategy." Scale to what's defensible.
- [ ] **[CL] Historical analogy** — "Throughout my career I have always…". Cut to the actual point.
- [ ] **False vulnerability** — unnecessary limitation disclosure. Remove unless gap is directly relevant.
- [ ] **Dead metaphor** — "hit the ground running", "bring to the table", "move the needle". Replace with literal description.
- [ ] **One-point dilution** — strong claim followed by weaker ones that soften it. Cut the weak ones.
- [ ] **[CL] Fractal summaries** — "In summary…", "As shown above…". Delete.

---

## Fabrication guard (hard fail)

- [ ] Every claim traceable to `01-candidate-profile.md`. Genuine gaps acknowledged honestly; no invented skills.
