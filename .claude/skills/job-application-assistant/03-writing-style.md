# Writing Style Guide

## Critical Rules

1. **No em-dashes.** Use commas, periods, or restructure.
2. **No clichés or filler.** Cut: "passionate about", "great fit", "leverage", "hit the ground running", "drive results", "synergies". No false dichotomies ("it's X, not Y").
3. **No generic buzzwords** without a concrete example or fact.
4. **No apologetic language.** Not "I think I could" — "I bring X, demonstrated by Y."
5. **No unverified company claims.** Every company-specific statement (partnerships, products, technology, expansions) must be verified via WebFetch/WebSearch before inclusion. If unverifiable, rephrase or omit.
6. **Reframe emphasis, not substance.** Apply the **interview backtrack test**: could the candidate explain this bullet without backtracking? If they'd have to say "what I actually meant was…", it's too far.
   - **OK:** Reordering experience; natural synonyms; emphasizing one aspect of a broad role.
   - **Flag it:** Academic + industry experience merged into a single claim implying it was all industry; posting terminology used for adjacent work; minor experience brought to front.
   - **Never:** Claiming experience the candidate doesn't have; implying domain work they haven't done.
   - For "flag it" bullets: "This is a stretch because X. Keep, soften, or drop?"
   - If fit score < 50: warn before drafting that extensive reframing would be needed.

---

## Tone

Direct, first-person, active voice. Confident without arrogance. No buzzwords, no marketing copy, no LinkedIn-speak. Think: a confident person in a good interview.

- "I built X" not "a system was developed."
- Demonstrate, don't state: a specific example beats an abstract claim every time.

## CV Rules

- **No prose.** Every bullet is a standalone declaration. No connective tissue ("in this role", "which allowed me to", "additionally"), no subordinate clauses, no multi-part sentences that tell a story.
- Header blob: 2 sentences max, tailored per role, never repeating claims from experience bullets. Sentence 1 is the identity line from `01-candidate-profile.md`, verbatim — states what *kind* of candidate this is, never an achievement, never a generic craft trait ("data-driven", "customer-obsessed"), never "N+ years" attached to a domain, no tense mixing.
- Core competencies: max 3 bullets, answering "what you'll bring" from the JD — domain-specific hard skills only. "Stakeholder management" and generic role craft do not belong here.
- **Claim-type discipline** (types defined in the Claims Inventory in `01-candidate-profile.md`):
  - `capability` claims (evidence-backed) are the only source for competencies and profile framing
  - `exposure` claims (side-project or incidental tech) appear only inside the Personal Projects entry — presenting one as a standalone competency is a fabrication-class violation
  - `hygiene` items (Jira, Git, Figma, Scrum, roadmapping) are never printed anywhere on the CV
- Every bullet starts with action verb or bold category. Be specific: numbers, tools, outcomes.
- Signal over coverage: a screener spends ~30 seconds on page 1. Every generic line next to a differentiator lowers its perceived weight. When a line is questionable, cut it — don't reword it.
- Every line traces to a requirements-map row or the identity line; the top-3 highest-priority requirements must be visible in the top third of page 1.

## Cover Letter Rules

- **Max 200 words** (body only).
- **Not prose, not editorial.** Declarations that happen to be slightly more extended than CV bullets. Every word must have a role.
- The only framing is "what the candidate brings to this role." If that can't be stated, the application is wrong — say so.
- Not a CV repetition. Forward-looking: which tasks can they solve and how. Past examples back forward-looking claims; they do not replace them.
- Subject line: [Title/education] + [relevant keyword from posting].

### Structure

**Opening:** Role + connection to background (2–3 sentences). Approve framing with user before writing.

**Body:** Lead with the one hard transferrable skill framed as "I can do X for you." Approve framing with user. Methods, tools, knowledge. Bullet list (3–5) when appropriate.

**Why this company (place early):** Specific — named products, market position, or projects from research. Not their marketing copy. Don't force it if there's no genuine angle.

**Closing:** Brief, confident, forward-looking. No begging, no over-enthusiasm.

## Anti-patterns

**Gerund-as-subject:** "Turning complex data into clear reporting is something I've built" → "I built clear reporting pipelines from scratch." Gerund openers signal a missing authentic claim; fix the thought, not just the syntax.

**Language by role type:**

| Role type | Lead with |
|-----------|-----------|
| Technical / ML / Product | Languages, frameworks, architectures, data volumes |
| Domain-specific | Domain methods; technical skills as tools |
| Consulting / Advisory | Stakeholder communication, bridging technical and business |
| Leadership / Senior | Delivery, mentoring, independent project ownership |

**Multi-language:** Default to posting language. Cover letter must feel natural, not translated.
